My teaching philosophy centers on fostering an inclusive, supportive, and intellectually engaging learning environment where students feel empowered to participate, question assumptions, and develop as confident communicators. I strive to connect theoretical concepts to real-world experiences, encouraging students to think critically, engage in respectful dialogue, and appreciate diverse perspectives. I also prioritize students' holistic well-being, recognizing that meaningful learning occurs when students feel valued, supported, and challenged.

A central goal of my teaching is to equip students with the ability to construct thoughtful, evidence-based arguments and communicate them effectively across diverse contexts. Through teaching undergraduate courses in Public Speaking, Persuasion, Interpersonal Communication, and Interviewing, I have helped students develop confidence in public discourse, strengthen their persuasive communication skills, and become more reflective, ethical communicators. In my role as Assistant Basic Course Director, I have also mentored graduate instructors by supporting their pedagogical development, fostering effective classroom practices, and promoting student-centered teaching. These experiences have reinforced my commitment to creating engaging learning environments that empower both students and instructors to succeed.

Ultimately, I aim to cultivate lifelong learners who can apply communication principles to navigate personal, professional, and civic life with confidence, empathy, and critical insight.

## Positive Learning Environment

I believe that meaningful learning flourishes in an engaging, inclusive, and intellectually stimulating environment where students feel encouraged to explore ideas, take intellectual risks, and develop confidence in their abilities. My goal is to cultivate a classroom in which learning is an active and enjoyable process rather than a burdensome obligation. By fostering curiosity, collaboration, and open dialogue, I create a space where students are empowered to grow both academically and personally.

To make course content relevant and accessible, I connect communication concepts to students' lived experiences through contemporary examples, popular culture, and current events. I encourage students to identify their own points of connection to the material, helping them discover how communication theory informs the issues, relationships, and experiences that matter most to them. Whenever possible, I enrich the classroom experience by inviting guest speakers and professionals whose expertise provides students with practical insights into the application of communication principles. Above all, I seek to nurture students' intellectual curiosity by encouraging them to ask meaningful questions, pursue topics that inspire them, and engage deeply with the learning process.

## Connecting Academic Concepts to Real-World Experiences

I believe that meaningful learning extends beyond textbooks and lectures and is most effective when students recognize the relevance of academic concepts in their everyday lives. As a communication scholar, I strive to bridge theory and practice by demonstrating how communication principles shape interpersonal relationships, public discourse, and contemporary social issues. By helping students connect course material to their own experiences and interests, I encourage them to view communication as a practical and essential skill rather than an abstract academic discipline.

To foster this connection, I design assignments that allow students to pursue topics aligned with their personal interests and professional aspirations. I also incorporate examples from literature, film, television, digital media, and public discourse to encourage students to critically analyze messages, question assumptions, and examine communication from multiple perspectives. In my Public Speaking courses, I frequently draw upon narratives grounded in lived experience—such as The Pursuit of Happiness, the Netflix series Maid, and TED Talks addressing mental health and well-being—to illustrate the power of ethical, authentic, and persuasive communication. These examples demonstrate how effective communicators can inspire social change while maintaining credibility, empathy, and ethical responsibility. 

My classroom emphasizes active learning through collaborative group work, experiential activities, debates, discussions, multimedia presentations, and interactive digital resources. These pedagogical approaches encourage students to apply theoretical concepts, engage with diverse perspectives, and develop the critical thinking and communication skills necessary to navigate complex personal, professional, and civic contexts.

## Creating a Welcoming and Supportive Learning Environment

I believe that effective teaching extends beyond the transmission of knowledge; it is a holistic process that fosters intellectual growth, empathy, self-awareness, and personal development. I strive to create a welcoming and inclusive classroom where students feel respected, supported, and comfortable expressing their ideas, questions, and lived experiences. By cultivating an environment of trust and openness, I encourage students to engage authentically with course material and with one another.

I seek to model the kind of communication I hope to cultivate in my classroom. I approach teaching with authenticity, openly acknowledging that learning is an ongoing process for both students and instructors. I encourage students to provide candid feedback and view constructive criticism as an opportunity for growth. For example, I use anonymous exit tickets to invite students to reflect on concepts they found most meaningful as well as topics that remain unclear. This feedback not only informs my instructional decisions but also demonstrates that student voices play an active role in shaping the learning experience.

Creating a supportive classroom also means fostering intellectual courage. I encourage students to question assumptions, critically evaluate evidence, and engage thoughtfully with differing perspectives. At the same time, I emphasize the importance of respectful dialogue, teaching students to articulate disagreement with empathy, curiosity, and civility. By modeling these behaviors in my own interactions, I aim to cultivate a classroom culture where diverse viewpoints are welcomed, and meaningful conversations can flourish.

## Supporting Student Well-Being

I recognize that students learn most effectively when they feel supported both academically and personally. For this reason, I view student well-being as an essential component of effective teaching. At the beginning of each semester, I provide students with information about the wide range of well-being resources available both on campus and throughout the local community, ensuring they know where to seek support when needed.

I also work intentionally to reduce the stigma surrounding mental health and help-seeking. By incorporating relevant examples from contemporary culture, personal narratives, and public discourse, I normalize conversations about mental health, resilience, and self-care within appropriate educational contexts. These discussions reinforce the idea that seeking support is a sign of strength rather than weakness.

Ultimately, my goal is to cultivate a learning environment where students feel intellectually challenged, emotionally supported, and empowered to grow as communicators, critical thinkers, and compassionate members of their communities. I hope that the communication skills they develop in my classroom will serve them not only in their academic and professional lives but also in building meaningful relationships and contributing thoughtfully to society.

## Integrating Artificial Intelligence in Teaching and Learning

I view artificial intelligence (AI) as an emerging pedagogical tool that, when used thoughtfully and ethically, can enhance learning, creativity, and critical engagement. Rather than viewing AI as a replacement for students’ intellectual work, I approach it as a resource that can support exploration, reflection, and skill development. In my classroom, I emphasize that effective communication requires human creativity, ethical judgment, empathy, and the ability to understand diverse perspectives—qualities that technology can assist but cannot replace.

I encourage students to develop AI literacy by critically examining the opportunities and limitations of AI tools. Students learn to evaluate AI-generated content, identify potential biases, consider issues of accuracy and representation, and reflect on the ethical implications of using AI in academic and professional contexts. Particularly in communication courses, I emphasize the importance of understanding how AI shapes storytelling, public discourse, persuasion, and the circulation of information.

When appropriate, I incorporate AI as a learning aid to support brainstorming, revision, audience analysis, and exploration of alternative perspectives. For example, students may use AI tools to generate ideas, examine rhetorical choices, or receive feedback on communication strategies while remaining responsible for the originality, accuracy, and ethical dimensions of their final work. I design assignments that prioritize students’ own experiences, critical reasoning, and reflective engagement so that AI becomes a tool for deeper learning rather than a substitute for learning.

As AI continues to transform communication practices and professional environments, I believe educators have a responsibility to prepare students to engage with these technologies thoughtfully. My goal is to help students become not only proficient users of emerging technologies but also ethical, critical, and responsible communicators who understand the human values at the center of communication.
